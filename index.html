Weekly Music

Sounds to move you

About

As the weeks fly by its always nice to have music to take your mind of the stressors and lift your mood.
We present to you albums to get you thru your week.

Red House Painters

Roller Coaster

Mark Kozelek and co provide one of their strongest, brooding, melodic album of their career. Great road trip music with standout tracks including "Grace Cathedral Park"

Ryan Adams

Prisoner

Ryan Adams defies all expectations for his career instead producing one of his most exciting records of his long varied career. Veering from rock and roll to singer songwriter.

Megadeth

Peace Sells

This was also the album that pushed Megadeth further into superstardom, and gained them even more positive media attention.Dave Mustaine's vocals are not the best on this record however musically it lives up to the thrash sound I love.

Arcade Fire

Reflektor

This band's fourth album ditches all norm's. The band escape their indie rock past to produce an album full of dance able hits..

Wilco

Yankee Hotel Foxtrot

Wilco skew their brand of alt-country to new levels. This record is meant to be experienced not just listened to.

Bon Iver

Bon Iver

Justin Vernon takes huge leaps from his singer songwriter beginnings and pays off big with memorable, cinematic songs.

Karate

Unsolved

Karate produce their strongest album by spewing their own take on jazz guitar mixed with slow blends of rock

Past Weeks Sounds

Magnolia Electric Co

Trials and Errors

Jason Molina provides a live album that pays serious homage to Neil Young and Crazy Horse. Which is fine in my book!

M83

Hurry Up, We're Dreaming

M83 write cinematic songs that stick in your head. You've heard these songs in your favorite movies and shows, now hear the full product.

A Tribute to Chris Cornell

Soundgarden

Louder Than Love

The second album and the first album on a major label.

Temple of the Dog

Temple of the Dog

A tribute record for Andrew Woood
